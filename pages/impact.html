Community Stories

Real impact from real people in our Emancipation community

Ages 6-8 ⭐ Featured

7-Year-Old Stands Up to Bullying

After completing Heroes of Home, Marcus saw a classmate being excluded at recess. He remembered the Inclusion Hero badge and invited the child to join his game. His teacher noticed and praised his kindness, inspiring other children to be more inclusive.
Impact: The excluded child made new friends, Marcus earned real-world courage, and the playground culture began to shift toward inclusion.
Submitted by: Parent October 20, 2025
Ages 12-14 ⭐ Featured

Teen Starts Respect Club at School

Inspired by Truth Tribe, Aisha created a "Respect Revolution" club at her middle school. Starting with just 5 friends, the club now has 30 members who meet weekly to discuss healthy relationships, consent, and standing up against discrimination.
Impact: School-wide culture shift with reduced bullying reports and increased student-led advocacy for respect and equality.
Submitted by: Educator October 18, 2025
Ages 9-11

Digital Kindness Campaign

After learning about cyberbullying in Reality Rangers, a group of 10-year-olds created a "Kindness Chain" on their class messaging app, where each student shares something nice about someone else every day.
Impact: Reduced online conflicts, improved peer relationships, and a more positive digital environment for the entire grade.
Submitted by: Teacher October 15, 2025
Ages 3-5

Kindergarten Consent Champions

A kindergarten class learned about asking permission from Kindness Kingdom. Now, before any hugs or games, children naturally ask "Can I?" and respect when someone says no. Parents report the behavior continuing at home.
Impact: Early foundation of consent culture, improved peer interactions, and empowered children who understand their boundaries.
Submitted by: School Administrator October 10, 2025
